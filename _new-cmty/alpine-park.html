Alpine Park Calgary Real Estate and Homes For Sale

Alpine Park's design is people-centred, which will reflect in its ambience as it transforms into a living, breathing community. Its defining features include numerous walkable areas through parks that take connectivity to the next level and diffuse traffic, and wide paths set away from the streets for added safety. The porch-oriented homes exude a friendly feel. Interesting inclusions are in the works, such as fire pits and pathways that will be lit beginning at dusk.

Alpine Park Real Estate — a people-centered neighbourhood, exuding the ambiance of a living and breathing community.

Alpine Park is a New Urbanist development in SW Calgary known for front porches, paths set back from the street, tree planting efforts and innovative park systems. This design concept has produced a walkable neighbourhood, safe and pleasant communal spaces, connected parks and an 'amenity core'.

The area is close to Kananaskis. You can take off to the mountains on a whim. If you're not one for adventures or hiking, Alpine Park's parks and lookouts offer ample relaxation or activity. In addition, the community has a Community Association Site for the kids to practice their favourite sports and hone their bike riding skills.

Alpine Park — Real Estate Market News

Inspired by the natural and architectural history of Calgary with mountain, urban, and prairie influences converging, Alpine Park’s new urbanist design was built around a dynamic mix of housing choices that put people first, strengthening social fabric and encouraging neighbourliness.

Alpine Park launched seven of its 13 show homes in November 2021. The community is expected to be home to over 12,000 residents.

Amenities in Alpine Park — Live, Work, & Play

Shopping —

Want shopping to be as convenient as possible? Village Centre, Alpine Park's retail hub, is walkable from any corner of the community.

Want shopping to be as convenient as possible? Village Centre, Alpine Park's retail hub, is walkable from any corner of the community.

The amenity core will have its own high-street with grocery stores, coffee shops, spas and other retail establishments that promise excellent retail therapy. It will function as the community's commercial hub, making running errands and receiving the services you want a breeze. Arts and culture activities will give residents more recreational opportunities and further enhance the character of the community. Seasonal activities are sure to keep the atmosphere vibrant and the community spirit alive. The plush-looking plaza at the Alpine Village Centre, with outdoor spaces and restaurants with expanded outdoor seating, will likely be a popular hangout place.

You can also venture outside of Village Centre to meet your shopping needs. Alpine Park is served by Calgary's top shopping destinations: Shawnessy Towne Centre and Southcentre Mall. Shawnessy Towne Centre has 250 stores, restaurants and various businesses, including Walmart, Home Depot, Safeway, Superstore and the Cardel Rec Center.

Southcentre Mall spans over a million square feet and is home to over 150 stores, including a large collection of lifestyle, fashion and food brands and labels, and retail outlets offering the finest home decor and technology. Its excellent selection of top name stores and top-notch food court is a huge draw for Calgarians.

Parks —

A community served by quality parks benefits in many ways. Parks encourage a healthy lifestyle and provide a space for entertainment. They serve as clean and safe communal spaces for building and strengthening connections. Park playgrounds promote inclusivity and help kids work on their communication and socialization skills. In addition, they encourage better environmental awareness.

Fortunately, Alpine Park residents are spoiled for choice when it comes to green spaces for recreation. Fish Creek Provincial Park is located close to the area. The second-largest urban park in Canada has over 100 kilometres of paved, dirt and trail paths for walking, cycling and jogging, and turns into a snow-shoeing and cross-country skiing destination in the winter.

At Shannon Terrace in Fish Creek Park, residents can enjoy beautiful hiking trails, wildlife views and large spruce trees. The site has picnic tables and firepits.

Alpine Park is served by multiple purpose-designed parks. Pet owners can head to the off-leash dog park across Alpine Avenue SW. Central Park offers a large, lush lawn, ornamental plantings, a lookout, a family playground and a climbing hill.

Storm Park is a unique park concept based on the award-winning Dale Hodges Park that collects stormwater from upland residential communities into a large Nautilus Pond, where it makes its way to marshes, meadows and streams before being released, fully treated, into the Bow River. It offers trails, public plazas, boardwalks and viewpoint lookouts.

Skyline Pond is a picturesque, relaxing area for residents to enjoy. It is designed to resemble a tarn, a small mountain lake that forms in a hollow scooped out by a glacier. Skyline Pond offers scenic views of the City's downtown core and mountains to the west.

Canopy Park is envisioned as a ridgeline park and features a trail that brings visitors above the treeline and drops off into a playground nestled amongst a forest. There's a large public venue space for winter tobogganing, and pavilions that serve as a gathering place for residents to meet up and hold barbeque parties.

Midway Park is thoughtfully arranged to reduce neighbourhood traffic off Stoney Trail while making it easier for residents to get around on foot. It is yet another communal space for residents to unwind, get fresh air, bicycle and for the kids to run around or play Frisbee.

Do the kids like bicycling or other bicycle-friendly activities? The Community Association Site has a multi-use sports court where residents can enjoy winter skating, and BMX riding at the 7 km-radius Pump Track.

Kananaskis Country, Alberta's mountain playground, is 30-minutes away. The multi-use recreational area in the foothills and front ranges of the Rockies is home to parks, campgrounds and an ecological reserve. Head off to the mountains for adventures or winding down. Popular activities here include alpine and Nordic skiing, hiking, dog sledding, snowshoeing, mountain biking and fishing.

Interesting Facts about Alpine Park

Schools In or Near Alpine Park

Neighbourhood schools foster friendships in and outside classrooms. School quality and access are important considerations for families reviewing neighbourhoods to live in. Alpine Park residents can expect to be served by Future Elementary School, a designated K-6 site and Future Middle School, a designated Catholic K-9 site.

Housing

Alpine Park has been conceived with new urbanist principles of walkable streets, shopping in close proximity and accessible public spaces. It already has a wonderful canvas to begin with - Calgary's incredible natural beauty and history.

The community's homes lend to its diverse and vibrant surroundings owing to their mix of distinctive architectures. The residences are constructed in styles ranging from modern Contemporary, scenic mountain and Prairie to historic Craftsman, Farmhouse and Chateauesque styles. Rather than driveways and garages defining the frontage of homes, porches usher sunlight indoors, invite kids and family to spend time in the front yard, and make it convenient to exchange pleasantries with neighbours.

There is no denying that the community design has placed the human element at its core, bringing people together, while ensuring the connectivity of a city and respecting the natural surroundings.

Developer/Builder History

Alpine Park is a new urbanist community from Dream Unlimited, a leading real estate company that boasts some of Canada's most transformative projects. Its concept master plan was done by Calthorpe Associations, founded by Peter Calthorpe, a well-known leader in New Urbanism, winner of the J.C. Nichols Prize for Visionaries in Urban Development, and among the earliest board members of Congress for the New Urbanism.

FAQs about Alpine Park Real Estate and Neighborhood

  1. Where do I get groceries?

    Alpine Park is a community in development. There are plans for future convenience and grocery stores. Some of the amenities are scheduled for completion in 2023, and others in 2024.

  2. How do I get around?

    Alpine Park is a self-sufficient community with a number of walkable pathways and streets connecting you to the places you want to visit and the things you want to do. This community will offer unparalleled access to the rest of the city due to its close proximity to the ring road.

  3. What amenities are there nearby?

    Just about everything you can think of! Alpine Park's Village Centre is planned to offer a wide array of stores, restaurants and services. A future pharmacy, hotel, a variety of restaurants, convenience and grocery stores will provide all the urban conveniences you need.

  4. Are there any cultural amenities nearby?

    For your arts and culture fix, you can head to the Village Centre, which plans to host programs and seasonal activities all year round. As the community welcomes more residents, you can expect a larger variety of events, festivals and programs that make living in Alpine Park even more pleasurable!
