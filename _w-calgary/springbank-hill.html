Springbank Hill Real Estate — a short cool description here.

From the multi-purpose Westside Recreation Centre that has something for every age-group to the events, community garden and programs by the Springbank Hill Community Association, prospective residents are never short of options for socializing and recreation. Springbank Hill has a decent housing mix, from reasonably affordable homes to luxurious properties offering expansive views of the Rocky Mountains and wooded green spaces.

Springbank Hill Real Estate Market News

Springbank Hill is increasingly becoming a site of new residential and retail developments. Avi recently made its foray in the area with Widlflower Townhomes on 81st Street SW. The townhouse development is one of many multi-family developers developing land in Springbank Hill. Another is the market retail centre by Ronmor Developers on a 10-acre site south of 17th Avenue S.W. across from Aspen Landing that will include a Co-op grocery. Buyers seeking new homes in a well-to-do community in southwest Calgary can expect to choose from many options in Springbank Hill.

Springbank Hill Real Estate Listings

Established in 1999, Springbank Hill is a comparatively new community in Calgary. Chosen for its location overlooking the prairies and Rocky Mountains, Springbank Hill has become a mixed residential and luxury community, based under the large Alberta sky.

Amenities in Springbank Hill — Live, Work, & Play

Golf —

A number of golf courses are located within a short driving distance of the area. If you're an avid golfer, you can choose from the top-quality public or private golf courses briefly profiled below.

Pinebrook Golf and Country Club is about nine minutes from the community. The private 18-hole championship golf course features modern amenities, spectacular views of the Rocky Mountains and river valley, and excellent practice facilities. The fairways are highly walkable, while bunkers and water hazards spring up plenty of challenges.

Elbow Springs Golf Club is about the same distance away. The semi-private golf club has four golf courses offering beautiful mountain views and unique golfing experiences. Water hazards and challenging holes keep things interesting and a practice facility allows for a good warm-up.

Earl Grey Golf Club is about 11 minutes away. The renowned private golf club features a 18-hole championship course and a 9-hole par-3 course. You enjoy scenic views of the mountain, water and city as you attempt the challenging holes. The club organises competitive and social tournaments throughout the season.

Shopping —

Springbank Hill is close to major shopping centres and neighbourhood plazas, meeting your essential and high-end shopping needs more than adequately.

Westhills Towne Centre is a quick drive from the neighbourhood. The open-air shopping complex has grocery stores, clothing stores, restaurants, pet stores, professional services and a movie theatre.

Signal Hill is another popular shopping choice. With a variety of stores to choose from and just across Richmond Road from the Westhills Town Centre its easy to find exactly what you need when out shopping.

For a quick bout of shopping for essentials, head to the neighbourhood plazas of Aspen Landing Shopping Centre. The modern shopping destination is home to top retailers and locally-owned stores, including Safeway, Pet Planet and Shopper's Drug Mart.

Richmond Square is located adjacent to Canadian Tire and Glenbrook Plaza. It houses a HomeSense, Save-On-Foods and GoodLife Fitness. Located on Sarcee Trail, the plaza is accessible off Richmond Road via 51st Street.

Amenities closest to the Springbank Hill neighbourhood include:

  • The Sweat Lab
  • Safeway

Foodies have an exciting choice of bakeries and restaurants that are a short drive from the area. A Ladybug Bakery and Cafe in Aspen Woods is only a 4-minute drive. It is popular for lemon tarts, coffees and crpes. The kid-friendly bakery offers gluten free and vegan options.

Parks and Recreation —

Proximity to parks is a major advantage of living in Springbank Hill. You're never short of recreational opportunities, all within walking distance of the community.

  • Springbank Hill Community Park is literally at your doorstep, and features a playground and trail
  • Spring Willow Terrace SW Playground has a playground
  • Springborough Blvd SW Park has a playground, sports field and ball diamond
  • Springbank Way SW Park has two playgrounds and a hiking trail

Westside Recreation Centre caters to the recreational needs of all family members. It has an aquatic park, fitness centre, youth wellness centre, climbing wall, leisure ice arena, hockey arena, fitness studio, cycle studio, gyms, skate park and specialty areas. Families can make the most of the facility's child-minding services, adults can sign up for group exercises, and youth can take part in activities and volunteer programs designed for their age group.

General feel and vibe — What's it like living in Springbank Hill?

A walk or drive in Springbank Hill reveals its abundance of green spaces and the luxurious homes scattered about the area. It is a fine example of suburban living while being close to modern amenities and public transportation.

Demographics

In 2019, Springbank Hill had a population of 9,943. The median age is 39 years. Married couples and families form the majority of the population. Compared to the City, Springbank Hill has a higher percentage of residents with a university degree, diploma or certificate. Roughly 43% of household incomes is above $200,000.

Housing

Many of the homes in the area were built after 2001. Single-detached homes dominate and there are fewer semi-detached dwellings. The area also has plush condominiums and townhouses. A perk of being a residence here is the stunning views of mountains and green spaces you enjoy from the comfort of your home.

FAQs about Springbank Hill Real Estate and Neighborhood

  1. What services does the community association offer?

    The Springbank Hill Community Association operates two outdoor rinks, one at the park on Springbank Blvd SW, and other in the Summit of Montreux. It offers soccer and t-ball programs for kids, and community gardens inside the Springbank Hill Community Park. The association organises free events for residents of all ages throughout the year, including Easter Egg hunts, Stampede Breakfast and Halloween parties.

  2. Are there any nearby gyms?

    The Springbank Hill community is served by The Sweat Lab, which offers spin, barre, circuit and yoga classes. The gym believes in leveraging cross-training for a healthy body and mind. Renewed Balance offers personalized fitness programs, nutrition plans, life or executive sessions, physiotherapists, massage therapists and sports medicine specialists.

  3. How do I get around?

    Quick access to a public transit system makes getting around convenient and cost-effective. The 69 Street station, the sixth and terminal station on the West LRT line, is in Springbank Hill. There are also several bus routes running through the neighbourhood.

  4. Will the kids have access to extracurricular activities?

    Kids enjoy plenty of opportunities for fun and games at the playgrounds, ball diamonds and sports fields in the community gardens. Skating rinks at Springbank Blvd SW, and other in the Summit of Montreaux, and the climbing walls, aquatic park and specialty areas at Westside Recreational Centre facilitate a vast array of fun activities.
