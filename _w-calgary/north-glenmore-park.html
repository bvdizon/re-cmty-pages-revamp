North Glenmore Park Real Estate — is a sought-after residential neighbourhood in southwest Calgary.

North Glenmore Park is a sought-after residential neighbourhood in southwest Calgary, bound by 50 Avenue SW to the north, Earl Grey Golf Club to the south, Glenmore Athletic Park to the east and Crowchild Trail to the west. Based on schools, amenities, and recreation North Glenmore Park shines as a family-friendly neighbourhood. Many of the homes are single-family residences built in the 50s and 60s. However, you can also explore condominiums and apartments here.

North Glenmore Park Real Estate Market News

North Glenmore Park is among Calgary's desirable neighbourhoods. Its home values have risen, and continual redevelopment works and improvements in road connectivity and urban amenities are expected to enhance the community's status, attracting more families and professionals.

Amenities in North Glenmore Park — Live, Work, & Play

Golf —

North Glenmore Park more than adequately serves the needs of golfing enthusiasts. Whether you would like to tee off at a public course or sign-up to be a member at a private golf club, you have some excellent options to review! Here's a look at a few:

Earl Grey Golf Club is a premier private club offering an exceptional golfing experience. Its 18-hole championship course and a 9-hole par-3 course are walkeable and treat you to great views of the mountains, water and city. The club organises competitive and social tournaments throughout the season.

Lakeview Golf Course is a 9-hole public golf course that challenges you even if it doesn't have sand traps. If you're a beginner or intermediate golfer, the greens offer a good training ground. Playing at a public golf course is economical and you can tee off at your own schedule. Lakeview Golf Course offers attractive family rates, so you can tee off with loved ones as often as you'd like!

Residents of North Glenmore Park have the privilege of experiencing world-class golf at Calgary Golf and Country Club. Its historic 18-hole course is laid out on a 126-acre site on the banks of Elbow River. The private golf club is an oasis for golf fans looking for an immersive experience. One of the reasons, besides the beauty of the legacy club and spectacular greens, is the rule banning the audible use of cell phones and other communication devices on club property. As a member, you can also make the most of the club's excellent buffet and staff service.

Shopping —

North Glenmore Park is close to shopping centres and amenities that make life simpler and more enjoyable. Whether you like to unwind with friends over coffee or shop for one-off items, you'll be happy with the urban conveniences available to you.

Chinook Centre: The modern shopping complex has stores to suit everyone's tastes, including popular chains and high-end department stores. From books, stationery, apparel, jewellery, toys and electronics to specialty foods, homeware, lottery tickets, sporting goods and health and beauty items, the mall is a one-stop destination for all kinds of purchases. It has a cinema and a busy food court. Providing easy access through Glenmore Trail, Chinook Centre offers ground and underground parking, and great year-round deals.

Lakeview Plaza: Located on Crowchild Trail and serving the neighbourhoods of North Glenmore Park and Lakeview, the plaza has a vast variety of shops and services. It houses an IGA, Scotiabank, Shoppers Drug Mart, pet store, dentist, bakery and florist, to name some. The plaza is popular for mouth-watering Italian fare at Bella Roma Restaurant & Pub. Attractive landscaping, benches and wide walkaways make Lakeview Plaza a pleasant place to hang out.

With two quality shopping centres providing the convenience you need, the shorter commutes save you tons of time a week for other activities. For leisure, you and yours can head to the nearby parks and engage in a number of outdoor recreational activities, as discussed below.

Parks and Recreation —

One of the perks of living in North Glenmore Park is access to a variety of green spaces and recreational spots. You and your family members have many opportunities to indulge in your favourite games, enjoy outings and socialise with community members.

Glenmore Reservoir and Dam: Calgarians' main drinking water source also offers opportunities for water activities except for swimming and anything else that involves entering the lake. Fishing, rowing, kayaking or taking a paddle boat on the river are allowed. You can enter the reservoir through the docks and boat launch ramps.

Parks are great meeting points for community members. Whether you're looking to play sports or bringing your kids for their daily dose of recreation and physical activities, the parks at North Glenmore won't disappoint.

Glenmore Athletic Park is located next to Glenmore Aquatic & Fitness Centre. It is a sports hub that hosts training, leagues and tournaments covering soccer, football, softball, tennis and running. The park has sport fields, ball diamonds, tennis courts, a velodrome, a Class A 400 metre track and field facility. It also hosts minor and adult ice sport leagues. Other amenities here include a fitness/weight room and sauna.

Families can bring their kids to the nearby 53 Ave SW Playground. Cyprus Villas SW Playground offers more choice, featuring a playground, basketball court and picnic facilities.

Demographics

As of 2015, North Glenmore had a population of 2,400. The median age was 36 years. Residents had a median household income of $105,448, exceeding the Calgary median of $97,218. As of 2000, 17.6% of residents were immigrants.

Housing

Housing in North Glenmore primarily comprises single-family detached homes, while condominiums and apartments make up the rest. About 20% of the total housing is used for renting.

FAQs about North Glenmore Park Real Estate and Neighborhood

  1. What's the public transportation like?

    Catching a bus is quick and convenient as the nearest street-level transit stop is within walking distance of the neighbourhood. Crowchild Trail is western Calgary's major expressway, and an important north-south link. It intersects several residential streets within North Glenmore Park. By 2027, it will operate as a single freeway between Glenmore Trail and 12 Mile Coulee Road.

  2. What's the closest hospital?

    The nearest hospital, Rockyview General Hospital (RGH), is less than two kilometres away. It offers comprehensive acute and outpatient care, including emergency, critical care, urology and ophthalmology.

  3. What is the best place to go shopping?

    Chinook Centre, the largest shopping mall in Calgary, has about 217 tenants including restaurants, grocery stores, department stores and a cinema. It meets the shopping needs of even the most demanding shopper thanks to its designer stores, specialty retailers and unique choices.

  4. What services does the community association offer?

    The North Glenmore Park Community Association runs programs such as community playgroups, babysitting, lawn care, functional fitness and summer camps. Amenities provided by the community include an ice rink, open gym, ballet studio and bridge club. It hosts events and offers volunteering opportunities for adults and youth.
