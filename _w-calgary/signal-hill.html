Signal Hill Real Estate — an extremely safe Calgary neighbourhood that offers a suburban feel.

Signal Hill is a relatively large community situated on the western edge of the built-up area of Calgary. Although the area was annexed to the city in 1956, it did not start to develop until 1986. The primary form of development in the area is low density residential. Signal Hill is part of an elevated plateau which stretches west for a distance of approximately 7 kilometres. As a result, there are some excellent views of the mountains and the Elbow River Valley. The Canadian Armed Forces used part of the Signal Hill area for training purposes in the First World War. The regiments who trained in this area had their identification numbers set, using large characters and stone as the material, into the hill side.

The Calgary real estate district of Richmond Hill is split into 2 distinct halves. The East side is a prominent retail district with many big brand shops. The residential real estate district of Richmond Hill is comprised primarily of low-density single-family homes. The far southwest corner also includes new condo complexes. This community lies in the valley below Signal Hill, and backs onto the hillside where the Canadian Armed Forces regiments set their identification numbers.

Amenities in Signal Hill — Live, Work, & Play

Shopping —

You can satisfy your shopping, dining and entertainment needs at the strip malls and service-oriented centres close to Signal Hill.

  • Signal Hill Shopping Centre

    The mall is right by the highway and easy to access. It houses 60 stores and services, including grocery stores, restaurants, fast food restaurants and various retail outlets. Superstores, Michales, Winners, Lowe's, Tim Hortons and Montana's BBQ & Bar are among the large retailers here.

  • Westhills Towne Centre

    The open-air shopping complex is a great place to dine thanks to the sheer choices it offers. It also houses clothing stores, specialty shops, grocery stores, a Petlands, movie theatre and professional services.

  • West Market Square

    West Market Square has a good selection of stores, restaurants, coffee shops and a pub. It houses Sunterra, Starbucks and other large retailers. The shopping centre is a short drive from Signal Hill and parking is free and plentiful. It is close to CTrain as well.

  • Signature Park Plaza

    Established in the 90s, Signature Park Plaza is among the earliest commercial developments in Signal Hill. It has a number of business services, from healthcare to dry cleaning, restaurants and various other businesses.

Parks —

  • Battalion Park

    Battalion Park is a Calgary landmark overlooking the Tsuu T'ina Nation. The centrepiece of the park comprises of four geoglyphs totalling 16,000 stones and forming the numbers 137, 113, 151 and 51 in honour of the four battalions of the Canadian Expeditionary Force during the First World War. The Battalion Park Interpretive Centre features military monuments. The park's location atop the hill also makes it a popular spot for enjoying scenic views of Elbow Valley and relaxing with friends.

    Signal Hill's proximity to parks and recreational spaces makes it easy for residents to engage in their favourite sports and lead an active lifestyle. Facilities close to the community include:

    • Sienna Heights Way SW Park, featuring two sports fields and a ball diamond
    • Signal Hill Community Association has two basketball courts and playgrounds, a ball diamond, sports field, rink, trail, clubhouse, community hall and multi-use pad.
    • Signal Ridge View SW Playground has a playground and trail
    • Sienna Hills Dr SW Playground has a playground

  • Westside Recreation Centre

    There's something for every family at the Westside Recreation Centre, which boasts of an aquatic park, fitness centre, youth wellness centre, climbing wall, leisure ice arena, hockey arena, fitness studio, cycle studio, gyms, skate park and specialty areas. The facility offers child-minding services, youth activities and volunteer programs, and adult group exercises.

  • Signal Hill Library

    Libraries foster a love for reading and strengthen communities. Signal Hill Library is the much-loved community library where children and teens can be seen pouring over books and doing their schoolwork. The library features a children's space, teen space, study space and meeting rooms, and has books, movies and audiobooks.

General feel and vibe — What's it like living in Signal Hill?

Signal Hill is an extremely safe neighbourhood with a strong sense of community. It is upscale in terms of home prices and household income, yet offers a down-to-earth suburban feel that draws families and inspires long-term residence. In addition, the gorgeous mountain and valley views enhance the joy of living in Signal Hill.

Demographics

As of 2019, Signal Hill had a population of 13,395. Married couples and families with kids make up a large majority of the population. In line with its status as a wealthy Calgary neighbourhood, Signal Hill has a median household income of $100,844. The median age is 47.

Housing

Signal Hill mostly has single-family detached or semi-detached homes, and a good number of condominiums and townhouses. Single-family residences typically have stucco exteriors, range in size from 1,200 square feet to over 3,500 square feet, and feature three or more bedrooms and ensuite facilities. Homes built in 90s, when residential development began here, have been renovated and updated to reflect a more contemporary design.

FAQs about Signal Hill Real Estate and Neighborhood

  1. What is the best place to go shopping?

    Signal Hill is home to the Signal Hill Shopping Center. Here you will find a variety of stores including craft, clothing, grocery, restaurants and a variety of other services. Just across Richmond Rd is the West Hills Towne Center with even more options as well as a Cineplex Odeon – the perfect way to spend an afternoon.

  2. Where can I find a library?

    Signal Hill Library in Signal Hill Centre has a good selection of books, movies and audiobooks, a teen space, a children's space, meeting rooms, computers and printers. The well-kept, well-lit facility has friendly staff and is popular among the neighbourhood's younger families.

  3. Are there any nearby gyms?

    Just across Sarcee Trail in the Richmond Square shopping area you will find a Goodlife Fitness Gym – offering their members services like, virtual classes, personal training, access to a wide variety of fitness equipment and several different types of classes. Classes range from yoga to cycling and everything in between!

  4. How do I get around?

    While some of the amenities and schools close to Signal Hill are reachable on foot, driving is the more practical choice for most errands. But you can conveniently take the bus to get around and get to neighbouring communities and beyond.
