Discovery Ridge Real Estate — a charming Calgary neighbourhood located on the city's western edge.

Welcome to the luxuriant, and affluent southwest Calgary neighbourhood of Discovery Ridge, featuring luxury properties with expansive views of the great outdoors. Built on the banks of the river, Discovery Ridge offers residents magnificent views of lush green forests, mountains, and the stunning Elbow River. The neighbourhood is peaceful, located far enough from the busy parts of the city but close enough for the daily commute into downtown Calgary.

Discovery Ridge is home to some fantastic parks, and those who love sports can make use of the community's playfields. Golf lovers have access to three impressive golf courses in the surrounding areas, and shopaholics have a wide variety of shopping malls at their service.

Discovery Ridge is a fantastic place to bring up children as well. There are several schools in and around the neighbourhood and recreational activities after school.

The neighbourhood of Discovery Ridge is located along the city's western edge. It is bounded to the north by Glenmore Trail, the south by Tsuu T'ina first nation reserve and Elbow River. 69 Street W forms the neighbourhood's eastern border, and the city limits make up its western border.

Amenities in Discovery Ridge — Live, Work, & Play

Elbow River —

The stunning Elbow River threads through the city and flows at the edge of the community. The river adds to this community's scenic beauty. There are many walking and biking trails linked to the river passage, and the river is perfect for rafting during the summer months.

Mountains —

Imagine waking up every morning to gorgeous views of the majestic Rockies. That's what you get when you buy a home in Discovery Ridge. Discovery Ridge homes offer sweeping views of the western foothills of The Rocky Mountains that run a total of 3000 miles from British Columbia and Alberta.

Parks —

Griffith Woods Environmental Park is 93 acres of lush parkland within the community of Discovery Ridge. The park is a natural environment park running along the banks of the Elbow River and is full of intertwining paths and miles and miles of trails for the adventurous. Enter the park, and you will feel like you have entirely escaped from the humdrum of everyday life. As a community resident, you will have full access to the park for hiking, skating, walking, horse riding, biking, family trips, or walking your dogs. As well as this fantastic park, Discovery Ridge residents also have access to several well-equipped playgrounds for children. Some of these include:

  • Discovery Heights pond
  • Discovery Rise Playground
  • The Fort Playground
  • Discovery Ridge Playground
  • Discovery Ridge Circle Park

Discovery Ridge pond is a well-known bird-watching park in the western part of the neighbourhood.

Weaselhead flats and North Glenmore Park, two massive parklands, are not too far off from Discovery Ridge.

Shopping —

Cornerstone Square or Cornerstone Shopping Centre is the community's business centre on Discovery Ridge Hill SW. It has a veterinary hospital, restaurants, Montessori, dry cleaners, a nail bar, a barbershop, a law office, health and wellness clinics, and a pharmacy.

Residents of Discovery Ridge also enjoy the neighbourhood's proximity to more prominent shopping malls like the Westhills Towne Centre and Signal Hill Centre.

Restaurants and bars —

Cornerstone square restaurants include:

  • Javino for specialty coffees, delectable wine, beers, and unique foods.
  • Maharajah is an East Indian Cuisine restaurant that serves some of India's finest East Indian, Kashmiri, and Mughlai cuisines.
  • New Discovery – the best place to pick up a wide range of spirits, wines, and beers.

Demographics

According to the 2016 Census of Canada, 4,475 people lived in Discovery Ridge that year. There are approximately 1,620 private households in the community, and most of the families have young children living with them. Over 90% of the community is made up of Canadian citizens. Owners occupy around 85% of the community's dwellings, while the rest are rented out.

Housing

Houses in Discovery Ridge are mainly single-detached houses, but you will also find semi-detached houses and apartments. Most of the flats are high-rise buildings (five or more storeys). Most of the dwellings were built between 2001 and 2010. So houses are relatively new, spacious, luxurious, and fitted with the latest amenities.

Developer/builder/history

Back in the 1990s, when development began, the neighbourhood was known as New Discovery. It was intended to be a gated community, but that did not happen. Developer Mac Radford developed the community.

FAQs about Discovery Ridge Real Estate and Neighborhood

  1. Which is the closest library?

    Signal Hill Public Library is less than 2 km from Discovery Ridge, in neighbouring Signal Hill. It is a relatively new state-of-art library spread over 14,000 square feet. The facility has dedicated spaces for children, study, teens, and meetings. Free Wi-Fi is available here, and computers and printers are available for use.

  2. Is the neighbourhood safe?

    The total crime rate is 60% lower than the national average, and violent crimes here are 69% lower than the national average. Property crimes are 57% lower than the national average. You only have a 1 in 80 chance of becoming a victim of crime in this neighbourhood. This is a very safe and secure neighbourhood.

  3. Is there any childcare available here?

    Parents will be glad to know daycare is available for their children in the community. 2L Preschool is a daycare centre on Discovery Ridge Bay SW.

    Soular Child Montessori Froebel Development Centre is another daycare centre and Montessori school in Cornerstone Square on Discovery Ridge Hill SW. The school follows both the Frobel and Montessori methods to help children explore, investigate, and learn.

  4. How do I get to the airport?

    Calgary International Airport (YYC) is approximately 36.5 kilometres north of Discovery Ridge and takes about 30 minutes to reach via AB-8 and AB-2 N.

    There is another route via Crowchild Trail SW that would take you 36 minutes to reach the airport, although the distance is shorter (33 kilometres).

  5. Is there public transport?

    Discovery Ridge is a car-dependant neighbourhood, and you will require a car to complete most errands. The community has excellent and reliable public transport, and at least one bus route passes through the community. 69 Street CTrain Station is the closest LRT and is less than two kilometres from the community.
