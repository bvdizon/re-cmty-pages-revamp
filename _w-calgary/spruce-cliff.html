Spruce Cliff Real Estate — a family-friendly residential neighbourhood with a strong sense of community.

Spruce Cliff is a family-friendly residential neighbourhood with a strong sense of community. Its mature trees shade routes on wide streets. New residential developments are bringing the neighbourhood to the attention of homebuyers seeking a quality community offering quick access to urban amenities and recreational opportunities.

The homes in Spruce Cliff are situated on an escarpment. Condos for sale on the East side are graced with city views and overlook the Bow River Valley. A hiking trail joins Spruce cliff to the Douglas Fir Trail, and the Calgary bike path along the Bow River. Biking to Kensington, Princes Island Park and downtown Calgary takes about 10 minutes from this prime real estate market.

Amenities in Spruce Cliff — Live, Work, & Play

Close to malls, shops and services, Spruce Cliff offers all the urban conveniences you need. Walking, snowshoeing, senior activities, lawn bowling and golfing are among the numerous recreational activities you can enjoy. Parks, playgrounds and sports facilities support your needs for fitness and socializing.

Golf —

If you enjoy golfing, you can tee off at the nearby Shaganappi Point Golf Course, a popular public golf course serving the Spruce Cliff community. It features nine- and 18-hole intermediate courses, a well-maintained putting green overlooking the downtown, a 44-stall driving range, a clubhouse and basic golf supplies.

Shopping —

Multiple shops and services are located in the small community. More shopping destinations and amenities are a short drive away.

Westbrook Mall

Westbrook Mall is a popular shopping centre among Spruce Cliff families. Located at the corner of Bow Trail SW and 37 St SW, the mall has over 70 brand name retail stores and services. Its anchors are Safeway, Walmart and Jubilations Dinner Theatre. Westbrook LRT Station is a short walk from the mall.

Spruce Centre, the community strip mall houses, among other tenants, the popular family-owned bakery Pie Junkie serving a delicious and healthy menu.

Other Amenities

There is a variety of other stores and services along both Bow Trail and 17th Avenue, including Subway, McDonald's, Little Lebanon Pita Pies and Donair and Milewide Car Wash. Amenities closest to the community are:

  • Spruce Cliff Grocery
  • The nearest fire station, Fire Station 8, is located less than 2 km away.
  • The nearest police facility, District 2 Office (Rosscarrock), is also less than 2 km from the area

Parks and recreation —

True to its image as a family-friendly neighbourhood, Spruce Cliff meets the recreational needs of its residents in many ways.

Bow River Pathway is a short walk away and features a hiking trail. A walking/bike route links Spruce Drive and Quarry Road Trail to Bow River Pathway.

Lawrey Gardens Park is an off-leash area and offers a great loop for walking. With the Bow River to the north and Douglas Fir Forest to the south, the park is a peaceful getaway for residents and also offers bird-watching opportunities.

Wildflower Arts Centre and Park is literally at your doorstep. The arts centre is just what you need to try pottery or express yourself through dance and fine arts. It offers school arts programs for students of all ages and custom programs for groups and kids' birthday parties. The centre's recreational services include a playground, community centre, craft room, gallery and three basketball courts. It also hosts an annual Greek festival.

If you're more into lawn bowling than golf, you'll be living in a community close to two of the best greens in Canada, at the Calgary Lawn Bowling Club. The non-profit club has a world-class facility for lawn bowling and caters to enthusiasts of all ages and abilities. It organizes several events throughout the calendar year, including social bowls, weekend jitneys, 2-4-2 league, junior programs and other social and competitive playing events.

Douglas Fir Trail and Quarry Trail and Bow River Pathway Loop are popular spots for hiking, jogging, walking and snowshoeing. The former is a 4.3 kilometre back trail accessible all year round and offering a slew of activity options. It is open to dogs but they must be kept on a leash. The trail is scenic and a bit challenging, offering stairs, bridges, lots of shade and nice viewpoints. It is particularly favoured by beginners and can get busy in the evenings and on weekends. The trail looks especially beautiful during the fall when the changing colours of foliage are a sight to behold.

Quarry Trail and Bow River Pathway is a 15.4-kilometre loop used mainly for walking and running. The trail offers scenic views of the landscape, excellent tree coverage and three bridges to help you in your endeavour. It is rated as moderate but does see bikers and a bit of traffic.

Come winter, you have the chance to experience the season's oldest sport - cross-country skiing at the Shaganappi Point Golf Course. Residents relish this experience under the city's sunny blue skies.

Demographics

As of 2019, the population of Spruce Cliff was 4,673. The median household income here is $42,025 while the median age is 38. Married couples and families with kids at home make up a majority of the population. About 28% of residents are immigrants.

Housing

The housing mix in Spruce Cliff comprises detached homes, condominiums and townhouses. Copperwood condo complex from Sandalwood Developments is a multi-phase development with high-end floor plans and incredible views of the Bow River Valley and northeast region. The other residential development here is the Westgate Park Condominium of multifamily units ranging from budget condos to expensive apartments with excellent amenities.

FAQs about Spruce Cliff Real Estate and Neighborhood

  1. What is the best place to go shopping?

    Westbrook Mall is close to Spruce Cliff. From grocery stores to coffee shops and restaurants, the mall is a convenient place to shop for essentials and eat out. While the mall offers plentiful parking, you have the option to ditch your wheels and catch a CTrain at the nearby LRT station.

  2. Will the kids have access to extracurricular activities?

    One of the biggest draws of Spruce Cliff is the easy access to parks and sports amenities that are well-equipped and provide variety in terms of the type of sport and recreational activities. Playgrounds, ball diamonds, tennis courts, visual and performing arts programs keep the kids engaged and happy.

  3. What's the closest hospital?

    The Foothills Medical Centre is at a distance of 2 kilometres from Spruce Cliff. It provides comprehensive healthcare facilities, including a 24/7 emergency department.

  4. Is there a community garden?

    Spruce Cliff does not currently have a community garden but the active community association does maintain some beautiful planters and flower beds in the community and are always on the lookout for volunteers with green thumbs.
