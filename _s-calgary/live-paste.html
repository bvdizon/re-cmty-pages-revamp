Pump Hill Real Estate Homes & Market — A History

Originally encompassed within the community of Palliser, Pump Hill became an independent neighborhood of the South west Calgary only in 1991. The community is bounded by the 19 Street W on the west, the Southland Drive on the south, the 14 Street W on the east, and the Glenmore Reservoir and the Heritage Drive on the north.

Home to some of the wealthiest families of Calgary, the community in Pump Hill is a great neighborhood to enjoy stress-free living amidst nature. Renowned for its luxury single family units and estate homes, the Pump Hill real Estate was named after the windmill that once provided water to the early settlers’ farms in the area.

Homes for Sale in Pump Hill are typically listed at a starting price of around the late 600s, with a few units being assessed at as high as just under the $20 million mark! Tucked away in a natural setting of massive open spaces and beautiful tree-lined trails, the community offers quick access to a host of modern amenities and recreational pursuits for the residents to revel in.

Amenities in Pump Hill — Live, Work, & Play

Lakes —

While there are no lakes within the community, Glenmore Reservoir is a stone’s throw away from the neighbourhood, and residents can use the reservoir’s facilities. Boating, fishing, canoeing, and kayaking are some summer activities you can participate in when you are a resident of Pump Hill.

Golf —

Golf lovers choose between Canyon Meadows Golf & Country Club, which is less than five kilometers to the neighborhood's south (approximately six-minute drive). Tsuu T’ina National Golf Course, a 30-minute drive southwest of the community. These golf courses offer beautiful settings, excellent golf holes, fantastic staff, service, and food.

Shopping —

Shoppers – here’s a list of shopping malls all within driving distance:

Glenmore Landing Shopping Centre on the other side of 90 Avenue SW is a 15-minute walk from the center of the community. If you live toward the north of the community, it’s a stone’s throw away. Glenmore Landing has 49 stores, restaurants, coffee houses, an animal clinic, a postal outlet, chocolatier, banks, multiple specialty clinics, and more.

For luxury brands and high-end department stores, you can walk into the sophisticated Chinook Centre shopping complex, about a 10-minute drive (approximately six kilometers) northeast of Pump Hill.

Southcentre Mall on Anderson Road SE is less than a 10-minute drive (approximately five kilometers) southeast of the community.

Parks —

South Glenmore Park is less than three kilometers (about a six-minute drive) from Pump Hill. It is a waterfront park that surrounds the south of Glenmore Reservoir and includes the Weaselhead Natural Area. The park is home to some fantastic hiking and biking trails, pathways, playgrounds, tennis courts, and a spray park and playground accessible to disabled children. The park is also home to the Glenmore Sailing School. Trees and green spaces also surround a musical garden.

Weaselhead Flats – the only delta in Calgary that features wildlife, hiking trails, pathways, and cross-country skiing – has many activities for Pump Hill residents to keep them occupied throughout the year. If you love the outdoors, you will love the wildlife and plant life that this natural park offers in the middle of the city.

Palisdale Road SW Playground on Palisdale Road SW in neighbouring Palliser is a lovely playground for little climbers and crawlers.

Dog owners can let their dogs off their leash at the Pump Hill Off-Leash Area on Pump Hill Gardens SW or the Haysboro & Southwood Off-Leash Area on Selkirk Drive SW.

Fitness —

F45 Training Pump Hill, located in Oak Bay Plaza on 90th Avenue SW, is a five-minute drive from the center of the neighbourhood. F45 is a time-efficient, full-body workout designed to burn 750 calories during each 45-minute session.

Anytime Fitness on Elbow Drive SW is a 24-hour fitness center that provides a total fitness experience designed to help you reach your fitness goals, day or night.

Restaurants and bars —

Karen’s Café on 90th Avenue SW offers both dine-in and takeaway services. Residents love that this café provides excellent prices, friendly staff, welcoming and prompt service, and quality food.

Demographics

According to the 2016 Census of Canada, 1,445 people stayed in private households in Pump Hill that year. Almost 60% of the population is made up of 15 to 64-year-olds. The next largest population is seniors above 65 years of age. About 15% of the community is made up of children between 0 and 14 years.

Almost 95% of the population comprises couple families, and about half of these couples have children at home.

The community has a small population (about 6%) of non-Canadian citizens.

About 73% of the houses are occupied by owners, while the rest are rented out.

Developer/builder/history

The community got its name from a windmill that pumped water from the landowner’s farm to the other houses in the area. Today, Pump Hill is one of the most exclusive and upscale communities in Calgary. While it started as a part of Palliser, it became a separate neighbourhood in 1991.

FAQs about Pump Hill Real Estate and Neighborhood

  1. Will I feel safe in this neighbourhood? What is the crime like here?

    If you are planning on buying a home in Pump Hill, you will be happy to know this is one of the safest neighbourhoods in Calgary. The total crime rate is 60% lower than the national average here. Violent crimes and property crimes are 69% and 57% lower than the national average.

  2. Which is the nearest hospital?

    Rockyview General Hospital on 14 Street SW is a large Calgary hospital serving over 220,000 people every year. This full-fledged self-sufficient hospital is less than three kilometers from the neighbourhood of Pump Hill.

  3. Which is the closest RecCenter?

    Paperny Family JCC is a recreation centre on 90 Avenue SW that aims to create a warm and inclusive environment for everyone. There is an aquatics centre, fitness centre, tennis courts, gym, fitness/weight room, climbing wall, and a wide variety of programs. While Jewish values enrich the centre, it is open to everyone.

  4. Is there a pool here?

    Yes, there is a pool in the Paperny Family JCC recreation centre. The aquatics centre has a 25-meter, five-lane warm, saltwater pool, a hot tub, a steam room, and a dry sauna. The centre offers swimming coaching classes, open swim, lane swim, and aquatic fitness classes.

  5. Are there any cultural amenities nearby?

    The Military Museums, which is Western Canada’s largest tri-service museum, is less than a 15-minute drive from the community of Pump Hill. Closer still is Heritage Park Historical Village, Canada’s second-largest living history museum spread of 127 acres of parkland, on the banks of Glenmore Reservoir.
