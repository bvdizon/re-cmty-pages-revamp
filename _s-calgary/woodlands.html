Woodlands Real Estate — a small and ideal Calgary community for family living or retirement life.

Woodlands is a small residential community in Calgary. It is located in the Southwest quadrant and offers many delightful community experiences and boasts of a golf course. You can also access a variety of outdoor experiences that Woodlands shares with its neighbouring community of Woodbine and Fish Creek Provincial Park.

Fish Creek borders the community to the south, 24 Street SW on the west, Anderson Road SW on the north, and 14 Street SW on the east. Canyon Meadows Golf Course and Country Club is located within the community.

Fish Creek Park is a natural urban provincial park that protects the Fish Creek Valley. Most of the park is wooded and a non-profit society called the Friends of Fish Creek actively endeavours to maintain this natural habitat. They have programs you can donate to along with the various activities you can participate in as a volunteer. You can become a Park Protector by registering for their monthly donation program to contribute to the societyâ€™s efforts at conservation and protection of the park.

Along with the wonderful outdoor experiences that Fish Creek Provincial Park offers to the residents of Woodlands, the community members can also access world-class golf services and indulge in other sports activities.

Amenities in Woodlands — Live, Work, & Play

Mountains, Lakes and Golf Clubs —

Residents of Woodlands have easy access to the neighbouring Fish Creek Park, which is over 50 years old and holds significant value for community members. It has a huge amount of hiking, biking, and walking pathways, offering a much-needed connection to nature. The members of The Friends of Fish Creek, a society dedicated to conserving a truly naturalized urban park through community activities and awareness, often organize events that residents of Woodlands can participate in.

Some amenities of the Fish Creek Park include picnic shelters, group day-use areas, an environmental learning centre, and much more.

The Canyon Meadows Golf and Country Club stretches the whole length of the Eastern side of Woodlands and is the perfect place for families to spend their leisure time. This renowned golf club, which was founded in 1957 and spans over 200 acres of gorgeous terrain. It offers members a challenging game of golf as well as unique social activities.

Woodlands and Woodbine are both members of the Woodcreek Community Association. Tennis courts, skating rinks, baseball, soccer, and football grounds are all available in these combined communities.

The Community Association is very active and provides a wide range of programmes for people of all ages. A large community centre expansion has recently been completed, and it will serve as the neighbourhood's heart of activity.

Some amenities found in the Community Association and nearby areas are:

  • A variety of workout programmes such as yoga, high-intensity exercises, Zumba, dance, and even a karate club!
  • The community hall is ideal to hold large events such as weddings and receptions. The Community Association also holds seasonal and festive parties such as Halloween and Christmas here.
  • Residents of Woodlands can participate in outdoor awareness programs run by The Friends of Fish Creek society.
  • The society also offers outdoor education for kids. You can donate to this cause or one of the many other natural conservation efforts undertaken by the society.
  • South Centre Mall, Costco, the Fish Creek Library, and the Anderson LRT Station are all nearby.

General feel and vibe — What's it like living in Woodlands?

Woodlands is a small and ideal community for family living or retirement life. The community is tranquil and noise-free thanks to its quiet streets. It also has various recreational and leisure opportunities for families to spend quality time together.

The official community newsletter, Your Woodcreek Chronicle, covers community happenings and is distributed monthly to keep information current and relevant. It is delivered to every home and business in the neighbourhood to keep people up to date on the most relevant facts.

Demographics

The Woodcreek Community Association was established in 1976 with only Woodlands as part of it. Woodbine was later incorporated in 1992.

In 2000, residents in this neighbourhood had a median household income of $71,234 and 14.2% of all households were considered low-income.

Immigrants made up 22.9% of the population in 2000. Condominiums or flats accounted for 11.6% of the structures. 24.9% of all housing was rented and the remaining was occupied by owners.

The community's median age is 41.7, with two to three persons per census family on average and a near-equal gender ratio of 50% male and female.

15% of Woodlands residents are over the age of 65, compared to 10% for the rest of Calgary.

Housing

The community of Woodlands was founded in 1976 and has many properties surrounded by green space. Most of the available housing in Woodlands dates from the 1960s to the 1980s, when the city's development boom peaked. The predominant housing types in this region are single detached homes and townhouses. The area is recognised for its large parks, open areas and abundant wildlife.
