The Neighborhood

Dover

A beautiful and tranquil residential neighbourhood situated in Calgary’s southeast quadrant.

Dover Real Estate

Affordable yet stylish homes dot the neighbourhood while residents enjoy easy access to a host of amenities, recreation avenues, parks, and green spaces. With downtown Calgary and the international airport being just minutes away, Dover is the perfect place for families, busy professionals, and seniors.

At the northern border is 26 Avenue S while to the south, Dover is bounded by Peigan Trail. To the west are the Inglewood golf course and Deerfoot Trail.

Known FACTS about DOVER

  • Schools

    The Forest Lawn High School offers education for 10 through 12 grades. The Eastside Christian Academy and Delta West Academy are private schools accessible to children of families in Dover. The main elementary schools are West Dover Elementary, Holy Cross Elementary, and Valley View Elementary.

  • Demographics

    The population in Dover in 2019, as per the latest available data was 10,351 with a population density of 2,465 per square kilometer. According to the City of Calgary Community Profiles, the 0 to 14 years age group accounts for 16 percent of the population, while the 65 + age group accounts for 15 percent of the population. Most Dover residents are young families or middle-aged adults.

  • Housing

    In Dover, about 40 percent of residential units are single detached homes. The neighbourhood also has townhouses and small apartment buildings at different price points. The housing growth in Dover primarily occurred through the 1960s and 1970s. Most houses feature three, four, or more bedrooms. Two-thirds of homes in Dover are occupied by homeowners while the rest are occupied by renters.

  • General Feel and Vibe

    Dover’s calm ambience is one of its primary unique selling propositions (USP). With easy access to pathways and public green spaces, the neighbourhood exudes a quiet and tranquil charm. As a well-established community, Dover is close to the famous Inglewood Golf Club and Inglewood Bird Sanctuary. Affordable homes and accessible conveniences make Dover a great place to live.

Golf, parks, and playground

The community of Dover has a ranking of sixteen in Avenue Calgary’s 2020 rankings of Calgary’s best neighbourhoods. One of the reasons for this is the abundant recreational and outdoor avenues. The Inglewood Golf and Curling Club, a prestigious course on the banks of the serene Bow River is a 5-minute drive from Dover. It is one of the best 18-hole Championship golf courses in Calgary with superb conditions and stunning views. Bird and nature lovers can head to the Inglewood Bird Sanctuary located nearby.

Dover Ridge Close SE Playground is located at Dover Ridge Close SE while the area is also served by the 31a/32a Ave SE and Doverthorn Rd SE playgrounds.

Fitness enthusiasts are sure to find many options in Dover.

The Renegade Training Company is at 45 Ave SE, about 29 minutes’ walk from Valleyview Park. Dover Outdoor Gym is just a four-minute walk from Valleyview Park and is a great place to work out while breathing in the fresh air. Ascension Fitness is a conventional gym that Dover residents can access.

Shopping outlets in and around Dover

There are many shopping outlets in and around Dover. Dover Village, is located on 26 Ave SE while the Forest Lawn Shopping Centre is also located nearby. The Deerfoot Meadows Mall is just a short drive away which is one of the largest urban, open-air shopping centres in Canada. This shopping mall features over 50 retailers including IKEA, Nordstrom Rack, Banana Republic Factory Store, Bouclair Home, Moores and the Gap Factory Store.

The other places to visit for shopping include Mini Supermarket, Dover Square, Towne Square Shopping Centre and more.
