Brentwood Calgary Real Estate and Homes for Sale

Brentwood, a mature and tranquil neighbourhood in Northwest Calgary, was established in 1960.

The majority of residences in Brentwood were constructed in the 1960s, although newer, higher-density projects are attracting a younger population. Brentwood's popularity as a sought-after location for adults, young and old, stems in part from its proximity to shopping centres, eateries, and major transportation stops.

Brentwood Real Estate — a Calgary neighbourhood known for its proximity to shopping centres, eateries, and major transportation stops.

Brentwood is a residential neighbourhood in Calgary, Alberta, located in the northwest quadrant. It is bordered on the north by John Laurie Boulevard, on the south by Crowchild Trail, on the west by Shaganappi Trail, and on the east by Brisbois Drive and Charleswood Drive.

Brentwood does not include Nose Hill Park, as it is located just north of John Laurie Boulevard. However, the park is very close to the community, making it easily accessible to Brentwood homeowners.

Collingwood, Dalhousie, and Varsity are all nearby communities that enjoy the same breathtaking views as Brentwood homeowners. The community is a great area to build a life since it has housing alternatives for people from many walks of life.

You can experience complete peace of mind from being in a fantastic neighbourhood, and the ease of being in a terrific location when you live in Brentwood. The Brentwood C-Train Station, where residents can simply jump on the C-Train, allows residents to reach the city center quickly.

Northland Village Mall, which has over 81 retailers, as well as excellent schools and the lovely Nose Hill Park, are all nearby. The University of Calgary, SAIT, and ACAD are all a short drive away from the community.

Brentwood is home to several world-class amenities that homeowners may find enjoyable, including access to the community centre, several parks, and off-leash areas. The community offers easy access to several shopping, dining, and entertainment facilities.

The ease of access and availability of amenities for all age groups makes this an ideal location for all individuals. However, the community's proximity to educational facilities and shopping areas attracts a young crowd, making it an ideal place for investments.

Amenities in Brentwood — Live, Work, & Play

Parks —

Brentwood residences have a convenient location near the University of Calgary and large retail malls such as Market Mall, Northland Village Mall, and Brentwood shopping area. The community has a variety of playgrounds and parks that provides residents with plenty of outside space.

One of the most notable amenities that Brentwood homeowners can enjoy is the Nose Hill Park, located just outside of the community.

Nose Hill Park is a one-of-a-kind urban park in Canada. This 2700-acre refuge is a true source of pride and a terrific spot to escape to, with natural grasslands, historical archaeological sites, panoramic views of the city, and animals ranging from gophers to moose. 8 kilometres of paved paths weave through the park, connecting it to the city's regional trails.

Some other amenities you can enjoy in and around Brentwood include:

  • Sports facilities offered at the Brentwood Sportsplex, including tennis, skating, and pool.
  • Activities offered by the community association for all age groups, including bridge games for adults over the age of 55, dance classes, and fitness classes open to everyone within the community and also to people from other neighbouring communities.
  • Children's playgroups, skating, and other activities sponsored by the community association for families.
  • Hockey enthusiasts can enjoy the hockey program offered to residents of Brentwood through the Westwood Hockey Association.
  • One of the many nearby parks and off-leash areas to spend quality time with family and pets.
  • The annual barbeque that is sponsored by the community association for residents of Brentwood.
  • Enjoy swimming or soaking in a hot tub or other fitness activities at the Sir Winston Churchill Aquatic and Recreation Centre.

Demographics

Brentwood first opened its doors in 1960. Brentwood noted a population of 6,059 people living in 2,595 homes in the 2012 municipal census, up 0.2% from its 2011 census of 6,049.

In 2012, it recorded a population size of 2,090/km2 (5,410/sq mi) on a total area of 2.9 km2 (1.1 sq mi).

Condominiums or flats accounted for 15.7% of the structures. Out of all the housing in the area, 27.3% was rented and the rest was occupied by homeowners.

The community's median age is 36.1 years, while the median age for the rest of Calgary is 37.4 years. Brentwood has an average of 2.3 persons per family and an equal gender ratio of 50% male and female residents. 41% of the families here have kids as compared to 48% for the rest of Calgary.

Housing

Many of the structures in Brentwood are single-family detached homes, with the remainder consisting primarily of big apartment complexes and townhouses.

This area of Calgary also offers a diverse range of unit sizes, with residences varying from studios to 4 or more bedrooms. Approximately 60% of the residents in this neighbourhood are homeowners, while the remainder is renters. The majority of the housing stock was built in the 60s and 70s, when this area underwent its most notable development boom.

FAQs about Brentwood Real Estate and Neighborhood

  1. Where can I find a library?

    The Nose Hill Public Library is located in the nearby Nose Hill community. It is easily accessible to all residents of the Brentwood community.

  2. Which is the nearest hospital?

    Foothills medical centre is the nearest hospital and provides a wide range of services, including a 24-hour emergency department.

  3. Will I feel safe here? What's the crime like?

    The majority of crimes in Brentwood are related to vandalism and vehicle-related thefts, a lot of which can be avoided by installing home alarm systems and keeping vehicles and garages locked. As such, the community is relatively safe and has a crime rate lower than the rest of Calgary.

  4. Are there alternative grocery options such as Farmer's Markets?

    Grassroots Northland Farmer's Market is close to the community. It was established in 1993 by three women. They raised money for environmental issues through the market. Today, upwards of 40 full-time vendors sell baked goods, fruits, meats, and crafts at the market.

  5. Is there a gym nearby?

    GoodLife Fitness is located less than a kilometre away and can be accessed on foot. Residents also have access to the Sir Winston Churchill Aquatic and Recreation Centre, which offers several fitness facilities and is housed across the street from the Brentwood Sportsplex.
