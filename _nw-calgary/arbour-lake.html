Arbour Lake Calgary Real Estate and Homes For Sale

The mountain views, parks and pristine lake are a treat to the eyes. Walkways and biking trails beckon you to experience the scenic outdoors. Events, programs and people enjoying communal spaces point to the strong sense of community enveloping Arbour Hill. Arbour Lake is perfect if you're interested in quiet living and yet need to be close to urban conveniences.

Arbour Lake Real Estate — one of the best neighbourhoods in Calgary.

Incorporated in 1992, Arbour Lake is an established northwest Calgary community with many green spaces, walking paths and family amenities. The lake is a hub of several activities and many pathways crisscross the neighbourhood. The community is served by a high school, a middle school, a K-9 school, a library, a mall and bike trails. Beautiful mountain views surround the area.

The Arbour Lake Residents Association organizes events and programs year-round and across all age groups. The strong family-oriented feel is palpable everywhere you go.

There are various housing types, including retirement communities. Several well-known builders have residential projects in the area, giving buyers an excellent choice of homes across various styles and prices.

Arbour Lake is bound to the north by Country Hills Boulevard, to the south by Crowchild Trail, to the east by Nose Hill Drive and the west by Stoney Trail. It is only minutes from the LRT station, ensuring quick access to the downtown.

Amenities in Arbour Lake — Live, Work, & Play

Arbour Lake has urban conveniences and plenty of green spaces. Whether you'd like to spend a weekend shopping, dining or watching the latest blockbuster, indulge in adventure on the lake waters or go for relaxing walks or bike rides, Arbour Lake has got you covered!

Parks and Recreational Facilities —

Neighbourhoods with quality parks benefit in many ways. It costs residents nothing to play and socialize in parks. The kids have a place to be active and develop social skills. By serving as a gathering place for residents, parks help build connections and community spirit. Well-maintained parks help keep the air clean and fresh. Parks used by many people also do their bit to create a safer environment as there are more eyes on the streets.

Arbour Lake residents have a good choice of parks and recreational facilities. Arbour Lake Residents Association has a playground and offers outdoor programs year-round, such as skating and paddle-boarding lessons. This is in addition to events and programs for all age groups.

Playgrounds nearby, located within walking distance, are great for families with kids. There's one on Arbour Crest Road and another on Arbour Vista Terrace.

The Lake —

The community lake is the single-biggest recreational zone for residents.

The lake offers a variety of recreational opportunities, including swimming, boating, fishing and ice-skating. It is full of rainbow trout and features pathways for strolling. The man-made lake is impeccably maintained and its pristine waters beckon residents of all ages. Apart from facilitating an active lifestyle, the lake offers gathering opportunities and helps maintain a strong sense of community. Unsurprisingly, the lake is a huge draw for home buyers as they have the privilege and benefits of living in a beautiful lakefront community, one of eight in Calgary.

Crowfoot YMCA is situated in the southeast corner of the community. It features a running track, training pool, swimming pool, fitness studio, gym, steam rooms and other amenities.

Walking and biking trails —

Arbour Lake is brimming with green spaces, its ravines and greenbelts invite you to spend more time outdoors with the sky right above you! A bike and walking trail winds through the hills and ravines, offering panoramic views of the Rockies to the west.

Crowfoot Library —

Crowfoot Library, the neighbourhood library, is large and nicely maintained. It has a children's space, teen space, computers and printers, and a good selection of books, music, audiobooks and more. As a bonus, you can enjoy scenic views of the Rocky Mountains from the comfort of your chair.

Shopping —

Arbour Lake is an established community close to amenities and facilities that make life convenient and enjoyable. For retail therapy, there's Crowfoot Crossing, one of Calgary's largest outdoor malls with more than 240 shops and services. The mall has shopping, dining and entertainment options, big box stores, boutiques and Cineplex Odeon Theatres. Services include Lowes, Safeway, Chapters Books, Keg, Brewsters, Chicken on the Way, Joey Crowfoot and Calgary Co-op, and The Spa Ritual.

Apart from grocery stores, you can also stock your pantry with products from speciality stores like Amaranth Whole Foods Market. Whether you're looking to unwind with a pampering spa session, enjoy gastronomic delights, catch the latest blockbuster or run errands, Arbour Lake has you covered.

Want to explore options outside Arbour Lake? Access to the downtown is quick via the Crowfoot LRT Station.

Demographics

As per the 2016 census, Arbour Lake had a population of 10,760. Median household income was $109, 790. Immigrants represented 36% of the population.

There was an increase in the number of people moving to Arbour Lake between 2011-2016. The population here is projected to continue rising.

Housing

Arbour Lake has single-family homes, waterfront condos and communities for adults 55 and over. The choice of single-detached homes and row houses is especially vast. About 90% of the homes are owner-occupied while 10% are rented. Most of the homes were built between 1990 and 2000 when it experienced its biggest construction boom.

Builder/Developer History

Many home builders have residential projects in Arbour Lake. You have a good choice of well-known builders to consider when buying or investing in a home here.

FAQs about Arbour Lake Real Estate and Neighborhood

  1. Where can I find a library?

    Love to catch up on your reading at the library? Do the kids need to pour over books and reference material for their school project? Head to Crowfoot Library, which has a large selection of books, movies, music and audiobooks. Settle comfortably in the study room or individual chairs - there's ample seating area and mountain views to boot! The kid's area has a sensory walled room, play tables with toys, a puppet theatre, blackboard and whiteboard walls, and more.

  2. Who has access to the lake?

    Arbour Lake residents have access to the lake and all activities on it, including swimming, boating, fishing, ice skating, curling and ice fishing in the winter. A lakefront pathway is a popular place for strolling among senior residents. During the summer, families enjoy picnics, kids build sandcastles and teens play volleyball on the beach.

  3. How do I get around?

    Arbour Lake has access to major routes such as Crowdchild Trail, Stoney Trail and John Laurie Boulevard. The CTrain station is ideal for those who prefer public transit and quickly gets commuters downtown for work.

  4. Will I feel safe here?

    Absolutely! Arbour Lake is a safe neighbourhood, with a majority of crimes dealing with vandalism and vehicle theft, and hardly any violent crimes. Violent crimes in the community are 25% lower than the national average. Arbour Lake is safer than 70% of the cities in Alberta.
